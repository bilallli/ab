01 — The Concept

Smart Value in
Premium Packaging

Pakistan's upper-middle class wants value without compromise. This is a first-of-its-kind organised dollar-store format in Lahore's elite gated communities — offering everything from kitchen essentials to decorative pieces, all priced between Rs.280 and Rs.850.

💡

The Gap in the Market

Lahore's posh residents over-pay at Hyperstar or Packages Mall for basic household items. There is no organised, well-curated "everything for a dollar" store in Lake City or Bahria Town. This is a white space.

🎯

The Value Proposition

Curated, aesthetically presented products at fixed low prices. The thrill of the "dollar store hunt" combined with the trust of a clean, organised retail environment. Quality perception through presentation.

📦

Vendor Advantage

With existing vendor relationships for good-quality items, you have a sourcing edge. Lower COGS means higher margins than competitors. Strong vendor network = ability to refresh stock frequently.

🏡

Captive Audience

Gated communities like Lake City and Bahria Town have dense, affluent, captive populations. Residents prefer not to travel far for everyday needs — your store becomes the go-to local stop.

03 — Location Strategy

Where to Open

Both locations offer strong foot traffic, but each has different dynamics. Here's the breakdown:

★ RECOMMENDED FIRST

Bahria Town Lahore

Higher population density, more middle-to-upper class families, strong community commercial activity. Lower rental costs than Lake City for equivalent footfall areas.

  • Target: Grand Bazaar, Sector C/D commercial areas
  • Footfall: Very high, family-oriented demographics
  • Rental estimate: Rs. 80,000–150,000/month
  • Competition: Low — no organised dollar store format
  • Size needed: 1,000–1,500 sq ft minimum
  • Proximity to families = high repeat visits

Lake City Lahore

More premium demographics, slightly smaller community. Higher average spend per customer but lower footfall. Good for a second store once proven.

  • Target: Sector M-1 or M-7 commercial strips
  • Footfall: Moderate, more premium clientele
  • Rental estimate: Rs. 100,000–180,000/month
  • Competition: Negligible in this format
  • Best as: Second location after Bahria Town success
  • Higher basket size expected per customer

07 — Customer Acquisition

How to Fill Your Store Every Day

01

Grand Opening Event

Launch with a bang. Hire a popular local food stall for the day, offer the first 100 customers a "mystery bag" worth Rs.500 for free. Partner with local influencers 2 weeks before opening. A strong opening builds word-of-mouth that lasts months.

Day 1 Word of Mouth Influencers
02

Instagram & TikTok Presence

Post daily "What's New" reels, unboxing videos, and "haul" content. TikTok haul culture is huge in Pakistan right now. Encourage customers to tag your store. Run monthly giveaways. Make the store itself photogenic — a neon sign, colourful displays, aesthetic checkout area.

Instagram TikTok UGC Daily Content
03

WhatsApp Community Broadcast

Build a WhatsApp channel from day one. Send weekly "New Arrivals" pictures. Bahria Town and Lake City residents are already in WhatsApp groups — ask admins permission to post. Offer exclusive WhatsApp-only deals. This is free and incredibly effective in Pakistan.

WhatsApp Zero Cost Community
04

Loyalty Stamp Card

Simple punch card: Buy 10 times, get Rs.500 worth free. This drives repeat visits and makes customers feel valued. Studies show loyalty programs increase repeat visit rate by 30–40% even in informal retail. Keep it simple — a physical card works perfectly.

Retention Repeat Visits Low Cost
05

Seasonal & Festive Campaigns

Create themed sections for Eid, back-to-school, winter, and wedding season. "Eid Gift Hampers under Rs.2,500" or "Back to School Kit" bundles drive impulse buying. Decorate the store for each occasion — it creates excitement and urgency to visit.

Eid Seasonal Bundling Urgency
06

School & Masjid Partnerships

Partner with nearby schools to be the go-to stationery & supplies stop. Donate small items to masjid events or community iftar in Ramadan. These soft marketing moves build brand goodwill and word-of-mouth in tight-knit community environments far better than paid ads.

Community Schools Brand Goodwill
07

Referral Discount Program

"Bring a friend and both get Rs.100 off your next purchase." Print referral cards at checkout. This turns every happy customer into a salesperson. In close-knit gated communities where neighbours talk, this spreads like wildfire. Cost is minimal vs. acquisition value.

Referral Viral Growth Low Budget

08 — Operations Plan

Running It Well

🏪 Store Setup

  • Minimum 1,000 sq ft retail floor
  • Open, aisle-based layout
  • Clear category signage overhead
  • Bright LED lighting (makes products pop)
  • Instagrammable checkout/selfie wall
  • Separate gift-wrapping corner
  • Clean, air-conditioned environment
  • Safe, covered storage for stock

👥 Staffing

  • 1 Store Manager (owner or trusted)
  • 2 Sales Associates (floor helpers)
  • 1 Cashier (trained, friendly)
  • Part-time stock handler (evenings)
  • Staff in clean uniform + name badge
  • Weekly product knowledge training
  • Incentive for upselling premium zone
  • Open: 10am–10pm (peak: evenings)

📦 Inventory & Vendors

  • Source from your existing vendors first
  • Also explore: Jodia Bazaar, Anarkali
  • Import channel: China via Alibaba/agents
  • Refresh 15–20% of stock monthly
  • Track slow vs. fast movers weekly
  • Never let shelves look empty
  • Seasonal stock ordered 6 weeks ahead
  • Maintain 4-week buffer stock always

💳 Technology

  • POS software (StoreHippo or local)
  • Barcode labels on all products
  • Easypaisa / JazzCash QR at counter
  • Accept all payment methods
  • Basic Excel inventory tracker (start)
  • CCTV with remote viewing app
  • WhatsApp Business account
  • Google Maps listing from day one

📊 Metrics to Track

  • Daily customer count
  • Average basket size (target: Rs.650+)
  • Top 20 best-selling SKUs weekly
  • Monthly gross & net profit
  • Stock turnover rate (target: 3x/month)
  • New vs returning customer ratio
  • Social media follower growth
  • WhatsApp channel subscriber count

⚡ Risks & Mitigation

  • PKR devaluation → review prices quarterly
  • Power cuts → backup UPS + inverter
  • Slow months → push WhatsApp deals
  • Theft → CCTV + staff vigilance + layout
  • Competition copies you → stay 3 months ahead
  • Oversaturation → expand before others can
  • Cash flow gaps → keep 2-month buffer cash
  • Bad vendor quality → test samples first

09 — Scale-Up Plan

Year 1 — Prove the Model

Open Store 1 in Bahria Town. Hit break-even. Build brand, social following, and loyal customer base. Document everything: what sells, what doesn't, what customers ask for. Refine operations. By month 12, you should have a playbook for opening Store 2.

Year 2 — Duplicate & Expand
